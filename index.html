Philosophy

We do not simply show guests the islands. We welcome them into them.

SALT is an editorial hospitality house rooted in Saint Vincent & the Grenadines. We believe travel is richer when it feels personal.

Our role is not simply to show guests the islands. It is to welcome them into them. Through food, conversation, landscapes, craftsmanship and unforgettable moments, we invite guests to experience Saint Vincent & the Grenadines not as spectators, but as participants.

Every SALT experience is curated with the eye of an editor and delivered with the warmth of a host.

Our ambition is simple: we do not want guests merely to feel that they visited Saint Vincent & the Grenadines. We want them to feel that they were welcomed into it.

Curated with the eye of an editor. Delivered with the warmth of a host.

The SALT World

A house, not a tour operator

SALT is a house of interconnected experiences rather than a conventional excursion company. Each experience is built around a distinct idea — flight, food, craft, night, privacy — and each is allowed its own name, mood and palette.

What holds the house together is not a single format but a shared discipline: the same typography, the same attention to layout, the same standard of hosting and the same editorial care applied to every detail, from the smallest printed note to the way a table is set.

A guest moving between two SALT experiences should feel a family resemblance — not a repeated formula.

Founder's Note

SALT began with a simple belief: that the most meaningful way to experience these islands is not from a distance, but through the people, flavours, landscapes and stories that give them life.

Saint Vincent & the Grenadines has long rewarded those who take the time to look closely — at a plate of food, a stretch of coastline, a piece of woven craft, a conversation that runs longer than planned. SALT exists to make that closeness available to more guests, with the same care whether the introduction is brief or the stay is long.

This is early, deliberate work. We are building slowly, in partnership with the people and places that already know these islands well.
