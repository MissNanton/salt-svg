The Vision

A sky the coast rarely lets you see

SALT After Dark is a refined stargazing and evening hospitality concept featuring dark skies, quiet landscapes, storytelling, considered food and drink, and atmospheric hosting. Away from coastal glow, the night sky over the Grenadines still does what skies used to do everywhere.

"SALT After Dark" is a working title for a concept still in development. Locations, timings and confirmed hosting arrangements are not yet finalised.

The best part of an island evening often begins after dinner, once the lanterns are the brightest light around.

Three Ways to Experience

SALT After Dark, in three formats

  • 01The Quiet Evening — a small, hosted stargazing sitting with light food and drink at a villa or private setting.
  • 02The Property Evening — a seasonal or occasional offering hosted at a partner hotel or resort.
  • 03A Closing Chapter — SALT After Dark as the final act of a longer SALT day.

The Evening

Settle. Look up. Stay.

Settle

The day's heat gives way

Guests are welcomed into a softly lit setting as the sky begins to change — the first pour arriving before full dark.

Look Up

A sky with room to breathe

With the setting chosen for minimal light interference, the sky itself becomes the evening's centrepiece, with light narration for guests who want it.

Exact viewing locations to be confirmed based on seasonal sky conditions.

Stay

No fixed end to the evening

Food and drink continue quietly through the night, with guests free to stay as long — or as briefly — as they'd like.

The Guest Experience

The guest journey

Arrival at Dusk

Guests are welcomed as the light begins to change.

First Pour

A chilled drink and a light introduction to the evening ahead.

The Sky Opens

As full dark falls, the setting's own darkness becomes the focus.

Light Storytelling

Brief, unhurried notes on the sky, the setting and the island's own night.

Supper by Lantern

A considered, simply served food and drink offering continues through the evening.

Departure

Guests leave whenever feels right, without a fixed closing moment.

For Hospitality Partners

How SALT After Dark could extend an evening

SALT After Dark suits properties with genuinely dark settings — a garden, a hillside, a stretch of beach away from strong lighting — and guests who would welcome a quieter close to the day.

What SALT After Dark May Provide

  • Concept and evening design
  • Hosting and light storytelling
  • Food and drink direction
  • Seating, lantern and setting styling

What a Partner May Provide

  • A genuinely dark outdoor setting
  • Guest referral and concierge sales
  • Kitchen or beverage service support
  • Weather backup arrangements

Closing Vision

An evening that ends when it wants to

SALT After Dark is a working title and a concept in development. We are seeking hospitality and destination partners with genuinely dark settings to help shape it.
