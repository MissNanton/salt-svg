The Vision

Made by hand, explained with care

SALT Studio gathers creative and cultural encounters built around local craftsmanship, art, botanical knowledge and the quiet satisfaction of making something with your hands, guided by people who know the material well — pottery, weaving, dye, garden and grain.

This is a concept currently in development, built in direct conversation with the makers and growers involved, rather than a fixed catalogue of workshops.

The best way to understand a place is often through what its hands already know how to make.

Three Ways to Experience

SALT Studio, in three formats

  • 01A Single Encounter — one maker, one afternoon, hosted privately for a villa or small group.
  • 02The Studio Day — several makers visited in sequence, paired with a meal.
  • 03In Residence — a recurring workshop hosted at a partner property.

The Encounter

Meet. Make. Keep.

Meet

An introduction, not a demonstration

Guests are welcomed into the maker's own space and shown, unhurried, how the material is worked and where it comes from.

Make

Guests take part, not just watch

A guided, hands-on portion follows — shaping, weaving or preparing something guests can genuinely say they made.

Craft offered depends on the maker available at the time of booking.

Keep

A piece that travels home

Guests leave with what they made — and with a clearer sense of the person and place behind it.

The Guest Experience

The guest journey

Arrival

Guests are welcomed into the maker's own workspace.

Introduction

A short, informal conversation about the material and its history on the island.

Guided Making

Hands-on time with the maker, at a pace set by the guest.

A Shared Pause

Where arranged, a light refreshment break in the same setting.

Finishing

Pieces are finished, wrapped or set aside to be collected later.

Departure

Guests leave with their own piece and a clearer sense of local craft.

For Hospitality Partners

How SALT Studio could extend a stay

SALT Studio suits properties looking for a genuinely local, low-key cultural offering — something guests choose rather than one that competes for pool or beach time.

What SALT Studio May Provide

  • Maker curation and scheduling
  • Materials and workshop design
  • Hosting and light interpretation
  • Packaging for finished pieces

What a Partner May Provide

  • A shaded workspace or garden area
  • Guest referral and concierge sales
  • Basic seating and work surfaces
  • Storage for materials between sessions

Closing Vision

Craft worth carrying home

SALT Studio is in development. We are seeking local makers, growers and hospitality partners to help shape a considered, repeatable programme.
