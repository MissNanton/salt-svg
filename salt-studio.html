Every destination leaves something behind. Sometimes it is a photograph. Sometimes a recipe. Sometimes something made slowly by hand. SALT Studio celebrates the artists, makers and craftspeople of Saint Vincent & the Grenadines, inviting guests to spend time with the people whose work continues to shape the cultural life of the islands.

The Vision

Made by hand, explained with care

SALT Studio gathers creative and cultural encounters built around local craftsmanship, art, botanical knowledge and the quiet satisfaction of making something with your hands, guided by the people who have spent years, often generations, learning their craft.

Guests might spend an afternoon with a potter, a basket weaver, a cocoa grower or someone preserving the natural dyes and recipes passed down through their family.

The best way to understand a place is often through what its hands already know how to make.

The Encounter

Meet. Make. Keep.

Meet

An introduction, not a demonstration

Guests are welcomed into the maker's own workspace and introduced to the person, the material and the story behind both.

Make

Guests take part, not just watch

A guided, hands-on portion follows: shaping, weaving or preparing something guests can genuinely say they made.

Keep

A piece that travels home

Guests leave with something they made themselves.

For Hospitality Partners

A meaningful encounter with local craftsmanship.

SALT Studio introduces guests to the artists, growers and makers whose work continues to shape Saint Vincent & the Grenadines, offering the chance to learn directly from local expertise while creating something to take home.

Ways to offer SALT Studio

  • 01A Single Encounter: one maker, one afternoon, hosted privately for a villa or small group.
  • 02The Studio Day: several makers visited in sequence, paired with a meal.
  • 03In Residence: a recurring workshop hosted at a partner property.

What SALT Studio May Provide

  • Maker curation and scheduling
  • Materials and workshop design
  • Hosting and light interpretation
  • Packaging for finished pieces

What a Partner May Provide

  • A shaded workspace or garden area
  • Guest referral and concierge sales
  • Basic seating and work surfaces
  • Storage for materials between sessions

Closing Vision

Craft worth carrying home

SALT Studio is in development. We are seeking local makers, growers and hospitality partners to help shape a considered, repeatable programme.
